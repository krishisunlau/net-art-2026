Silenced by Design

Curated by Krishita Laungani

This exhibition brings together three net art works that focus on data, power, and what is missing or hidden online. Created between the 1990s and 2010s, these works show that data is not neutral and often reflects what people in power choose to collect or ignore. Antoni Muntadas’s The File Room, Mariam Ghani and Chitra Ganesh’s How Do You See the Disappeared? A Warm Database, and Mimi Onuoha’s The Library of Missing Datasets all explore how information can be controlled, erased, or left out. A key connection between these works is their focus on absence. Instead of only showing data, they highlight what is missing and why that matters. They also use archives and databases in a critical way that questions their authority. By using interactive, web-based formats, these artists turn the internet into a space for questioning power. Together, these works show how net art can reveal hidden systems and bring attention to voices that are often ignored.


The File Room (1994–1998) — Antoni Muntadas
An online archive of censorship cases from around the world. It shows how institutions control information and what people are allowed to see.
